Family Governance Curriculum

Office of the Fiduciary – Educational Series
Structured educational curriculum for family trust governance, fiduciary administration, continuity infrastructure, and multi-generational governance doctrine. Authorized personnel only — institutional governance literacy.

I. Family Trust Formation & Authority

PRINCIPLE

Trust formation requires lawful purpose, documented authority, and intentional structure. The trust instrument establishes the governance framework.

CORE ELEMENTS

Settlor, trustee, trust property, lawful purpose, and identifiable beneficiaries. Each element creates obligations and enforceable rights.

EXECUTION METHOD

Draft trust instrument with: identification of parties, statement of intent, trust property schedule, distribution provisions, trustee powers, and amendment procedures. Execute with formalities required by governing law.

II. Fiduciary Duties & Trustee Obligations

PRINCIPLE

Fiduciary duty comprises loyalty, care, impartiality, and disclosure. These duties are non-waivable and enforceable by beneficiaries.

DUTY OF LOYALTY

Act solely in interest of beneficiaries, avoid self-dealing, refrain from conflicts of interest. Any transaction with trust must be transparent and fair.

DUTY OF CARE

Prudent administration, investment oversight, recordkeeping, and professional standards. Delegation requires careful selection and supervision.

III. Administrative Record & Continuity Systems

PRINCIPLE

Record precedes recognition. The administrative record is the exclusive foundation for accountability, enforcement, and generational continuity.

REQUIRED RECORDS

Chronological trust log, asset schedules, distribution records, tax filings, beneficiary communications, meeting minutes, and trustee decisions.

EXECUTION METHOD

Maintain bound log or digital archive with version control, date stamping, and audit trail. Never delete entries; add supplements and corrections.

IV. Succession & Multi-Generational Continuity

PRINCIPLE

Continuity requires documented succession protocols, trained successor fiduciaries, and institutional memory preservation.

SUCCESSION PLANNING

Identify successor trustees, define transition triggers (incapacity, resignation, removal), establish training period, and document institutional knowledge transfer.

FAMILY COUNCIL

Structured communication body for beneficiaries to participate in governance, receive information, and provide input on non-distributive matters.

V. Beneficiary Rights & Information Access

PRINCIPLE

Beneficiaries hold equitable title and possess information rights, accounting demands, and enforcement remedies.

INFORMATION RIGHTS

Trustee must provide trust instrument, annual accountings, tax returns, and material information upon reasonable request.

REMEDIES FOR BREACH

Beneficiaries may demand accounting, surcharge the trustee for losses, seek removal, or enforce specific performance of trust terms.

VI. Tax, Compliance & Regulatory Posture

PRINCIPLE

Trust administration must comply with tax filing obligations, reporting requirements, and applicable regulatory frameworks.

TAX OBLIGATIONS

File Form 1041 for estates and trusts, issue Schedule K-1 to beneficiaries, pay estimated taxes, maintain tax records for minimum seven years.

COMPLIANCE STRUCTURE

Engage qualified tax preparer, maintain separate EIN, segregate trust assets from personal assets, and document all financial transactions.

VII. Trust Protector & Oversight Mechanisms

PRINCIPLE

Trust protector provides independent oversight, modification authority, and trustee removal power as defined in trust instrument.

PROTECTOR POWERS

Remove and appoint trustees, modify administrative provisions, consent to distributions, and resolve disputes between fiduciaries and beneficiaries.

QUALIFICATIONS

Protector should be independent, possess fiduciary experience, and act in good faith for all beneficiaries impartially.

VIII. Dispute Resolution & Equitable Enforcement

PRINCIPLE

Trust disputes require equitable remedies — surcharge, constructive trust, accounting, or removal — rather than legal damages.

ENFORCEMENT MECHANISMS

Beneficiary demand for accounting, court petition for surcharge, motion to remove trustee, or action to construe trust terms.

ALTERNATIVE RESOLUTION

Mediation, arbitration, or family council resolution before litigation to preserve relationships and reduce costs.

Governance begins with structure. Structure requires discipline. Discipline preserves continuity. Continuity serves beneficiaries.
