EXECUTIVE AI GOVERNANCE // HUMAN OVERSIGHT REQUIRED

Artificial intelligence without governance increases institutional risk.

Kelly Legacy Estates approaches AI as a governance matter rather than merely a technology matter. AI systems extend institutional capability, but unmanaged systems increase operational exposure, procedural instability, data risk, continuity failures, and accountability breakdowns.

AI GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES
The Five Governance Requirements
AI-GOV-001

Human Review

No material institutional output should rely exclusively upon autonomous AI determination. Human review remains final.

AI-GOV-002

Record Preservation

AI-assisted workflows must preserve traceability, documentation, review logs, and administrative continuity.

AI-GOV-003

Authority Boundaries

AI systems must operate within defined authority scopes, operational permissions, and governance restrictions.

AI-GOV-004

Data Segregation

Institutional records, operational data, and governance documentation require disciplined separation and access control.

AI-GOV-005

Continuity Oversight

AI deployment must support continuity rather than dependency, preserving institutional resilience and governance stability.

AI-GOV-006

Procedural Accountability

Every workflow requires identifiable review procedures, escalation paths, and administrative responsibility structures.

FIDUCIARY AI STRUCTURE
AI Requires Governance Discipline

The Kelly Legacy framework treats AI systems as extensions of operational authority. Every AI deployment therefore requires procedural structure, review discipline, documentation controls, and governance boundaries.

GOVERNANCE POSITION: AI does not eliminate fiduciary duty. AI increases the need for disciplined fiduciary oversight.
AI-ROLE-001

Educational Assistance

AI may support education, organization, summarization, procedural drafting, and structured communication assistance.

AI-ROLE-002

Administrative Support

AI systems may assist intake routing, governance classification support, workflow organization, and continuity documentation.

AI-ROLE-003

Restricted Functions

AI systems do not independently provide legal representation, fiduciary determinations, financial advice, or dispositive institutional rulings.
